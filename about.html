Hi, I'm Subash

I'm still a student but I've spent a decent amount of my own time on this stuff outside of class. It started because I was curious about how sites like YouTube and Instagram actually work under the hood, that question sent me down a rabbit hole I haven't found the bottom of yet.

By now I can build a full page from scratch: HTML structure, CSS styling, Bootstrap layouts, JavaScript for interactivity. I'm also starting to connect it all to actual server-side code with PHP, which is where it gets genuinely interesting.

This portfolio is my way of proving I understand something, not just that I can look it up. Every section has a working demo because there's a big difference between reading about flexbox and actually moving elements around with it.

How I Work
Values &
Approach
🔍
Curiosity First
I want to know why something works, not just paste it and move on. Understanding the reasoning is what actually sticks long-term.
🛠
Build to Learn
Passive reading gets me maybe 30% of the way. The rest happens when I'm actually building something, even if it's a tiny throwaway demo that only I'll ever see.
📖
Document Everything
If I can explain it clearly enough for someone else to follow, I know I actually get it. That's basically why this whole portfolio exists.
🤝
Ask Questions
I'd rather look a bit confused for five minutes than be stuck for a week. Good teams run on questions, not people pretending they already know everything.
📱
Mobile First
Most people browsing from Nepal are on their phones. Responsiveness isn't a bonus feature, it's just part of doing the job properly.
🌱
Steady Progress
I'm not trying to learn everything at once. One solid concept at a time beats burning out halfway through trying to rush everything.
